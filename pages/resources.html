Resources to get you started

Below are resources that will help you learn how to code and help you get the basics down. We've picked 6 different websites and have categorised them into Free Services, Paid Services and Online Degrees. This will allow you to learn how to code regardless of your budget. All of them teach you the same thing, how to build a website using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. You're not missing out on much by going with the free options but, if you want a little extra value feel free to look at the Paid Services and Online Degrees.

Free services

First up we have free services. These websites will teach you how to code while being absolutely free so the only investment you make is your time. With free services you are completely in control and you are your only boss. Consider whether your able to motivate yourself to keep learning even when things get tough.

Youtube

Youtube is a great website for funny videos but also educational content. It's filled with a wide range of educational topics including web development all for the price of $0.

Khan Academy

Khan Academy offers a course called "Intro to HTML/CSS: Making webpages" which is designed to introduce students to the basics of HTML and CSS, the languages that are used to create and style webpages. The course covers topics such as creating a webpage, adding text and images, and styling elements with CSS. By the end of the course, students will have a solid foundation in HTML and CSS and be able to create their own simple webpages.

Paid Services

If you have money to spare and would benefit from having a mentor, you should consider paid services. Udemy and Skillshare are two reputable websites where you can learn a variety of skills. The advantage of paid services over their free counterpart is there is a a proper structure you have to following doing these courses.

Udemy

"The Web Developer Bootcamp 2022" is a course offered on Udemy that is designed to teach students the skills and knowledge they need to become a web developer. The course covers a wide range of topics, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, and more. It also includes hands-on projects and exercises to help students apply what they learn in real-world situations. By the end of the course, students will have a strong foundation in web development and be able to build their own websites and web applications. The course is taught by experienced instructors and includes over 50 hours of video content.

Skillshare

Skillshare offers a wide range of web development courses that are designed to help students learn the skills and knowledge they need to become a successful web developer. These courses cover a variety of topics, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. They are taught by experienced instructors and are flexible and self-paced, allowing students to learn at their own pace and fit the coursework into their busy schedule. Skillshare also offers affordable pricing, making its courses accessible to a wide range of students. Overall, Skillshare's web development courses provide a valuable learning opportunity for anyone interested in pursuing a career in web development.

Online Degree

An online degree in web development can provide students with the knowledge and skills they need to pursue a career as a web developer. These programs typically cover a wide range of topics, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. Students can learn at their own pace and fit the coursework into their busy schedule. Online programs also often offer more affordable tuition than traditional in-person programs. By earning an online degree in web development, students can gain the knowledge and skills they need to succeed in this growing field.

Coursera

"HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for Web Developers" is a course offered on Coursera by John Hopkins University. This course is designed to teach students the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, which are the languages used to create and style web pages. The course covers topics such as creating a webpage, adding text and images, and using JavaScript to add interactivity to a page. By the end of the course, students will have a solid foundation in web development and be able to create their own simple web pages. The course is taught by experienced instructors and includes interactive exercises and quizzes to help students apply what they learn.

Udacity

"Full Stack Web Developer" is a course offered on Udemy that is designed to teach students the skills and knowledge they need to become a full stack web developer. A full stack web developer is a developer who has expertise in both front-end and back-end web development. This course covers a wide range of topics, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, and more. It also includes hands-on projects and exercises to help students apply what they learn in real-world situations. By the end of the course, students will have a strong foundation in web development and be able to build their own websites and web applications from start to finish. The course is taught by experienced instructors and includes over 50 hours of video content.
